Chapter 31

A Thumb-print And What Came Of It

WE were approaching Napoleon, Arkansas. So I began to think about my errand there. Time, noonday; and bright and sunny. This was bad—not best, anyway; for mine was not (preferably a noonday kind of errand. The more I thought, the more that fact pushed itself upon me—now in one form, now in another.
