This began to bring in one straggler after another from the ranks of the new-fledged pilots, in the dull (summer season. Better have twenty-five dollars than starve; the initiation fee was only twelve dollars, and no dues required from the unemployed. )

Also, the widows of deceased members in good standing could draw twenty-five dollars per month, and a certain sum for each of their children. Also, the said deceased would be buried at the association's expense. These things resurrected all the superannuated and forgotten pilots in the Mississippi Valley.
